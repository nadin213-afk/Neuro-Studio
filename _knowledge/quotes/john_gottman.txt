JOHN GOTTMAN — Key Ideas & Quotes
Relationship researcher, The Gottman Institute
================================================

CORE IDEAS:

1. The Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse
   - Criticism, Contempt, Defensiveness, Stonewalling
   - These four patterns predict divorce with over 90% accuracy.
   - Contempt is the single greatest predictor of relationship failure.

2. Bids for connection
   - Partners constantly make small "bids" for attention, affection, connection.
   - Turning toward bids (vs. turning away or against) is the foundation of trust and intimacy.
   - Masters of relationships turn toward bids 86% of the time; disasters only 33%.

3. The 5:1 ratio
   - Stable relationships maintain a ratio of at least 5 positive interactions for every 1 negative.
   - This ratio holds during conflict as well.

4. The Sound Relationship House
   - Build love maps (knowing your partner's world)
   - Share fondness and admiration
   - Turn toward instead of away
   - Maintain a positive perspective
   - Manage conflict
   - Make life dreams come true
   - Create shared meaning

5. Repair attempts
   - The ability to repair after conflict matters more than whether couples fight.
   - Successful repair attempts are the "secret weapon" of emotionally intelligent couples.

6. Emotional attunement
   - A-T-T-U-N-E: Attend, Turn toward, Tolerate, Understand, Non-defensively listen, Empathize.

KEY QUOTES:

"In the last decade or so, science has discovered a tremendous amount
about the role emotions play in our lives. Researchers have found that
even more than IQ, your emotional awareness and ability to handle feelings
will determine your success and happiness in all walks of life."
— Raising an Emotionally Intelligent Child (1997)

"Happy marriages are based on a deep friendship. By this I mean a mutual
respect for and enjoyment of each other's company."
— The Seven Principles for Making Marriage Work (1999)

"The difference between happy and unhappy couples is the balance between
positive and negative interactions during conflict. There is a very specific
ratio that makes love last."
— Why Marriages Succeed or Fail (1994)

"Contempt is the sulfuric acid for love."
— The Seven Principles

"Successful conflict resolution isn't about one person changing. It's about
negotiating and finding common ground and ways to accommodate each other."
— The Seven Principles

"In a good relationship, people get angry, but the anger is processed.
Masters see negativity as a cue to do something different. Disasters see
it as a cue to fight back."
— Research talks

"Every positive thing you do in your relationship is foreplay."
— The Seven Principles

"Trust is built in very small moments. When your partner reaches for you,
do you turn toward them or turn away?"
— What Makes Love Last? (2012)

"People who have good relationships aren't smarter, richer, or more
psychologically astute than others. But in their day-to-day lives, they
have hit upon a dynamic that keeps their negative thoughts and feelings
about each other from overwhelming their positive ones."
— The Seven Principles

"The masters of relationships think about their partner when they are
not together. They are building a cognitive room in their brain for that
person. The disasters are not doing this."
— Making Marriage Work lecture

"A lasting marriage results from a couple's ability to resolve the conflicts
