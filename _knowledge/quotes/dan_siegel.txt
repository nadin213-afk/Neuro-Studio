DAN SIEGEL — Key Ideas & Quotes
Psychiatrist, interpersonal neurobiology pioneer
=================================================

CORE IDEAS:

1. Interpersonal neurobiology (IPNB)
   - The mind is not just brain activity — it emerges from relationships and the body.
   - The mind is a relational and embodied process that regulates energy and information flow.
   - Relationships shape neural architecture, especially in early life.

2. The Window of Tolerance
   - Each person has a zone of arousal where they can function and process emotions effectively.
   - Trauma narrows this window — leading to hyperarousal (anxiety, rage) or hypoarousal (shutdown, numbness).
   - Therapy and secure relationships widen the window.

3. Integration
   - Mental health = integration — linking differentiated parts of the brain and mind into a coherent whole.
   - Chaos and rigidity are signs of impaired integration.
   - "When we are integrated, we are flexible, adaptive, coherent, energized, and stable (FACES)."

4. Mindsight
   - The ability to perceive your own mind and the minds of others.
   - A learnable skill that strengthens empathy, self-awareness, and emotional regulation.
   - Mindsight = insight + empathy + integration.

5. Attachment and the developing brain
   - Secure attachment promotes integrative brain development.
   - The parent's own unresolved attachment history is the strongest predictor of the child's attachment pattern.
   - "Making sense of your story" is the key to earned security.

6. The hand model of the brain
   - A simple model to explain how the prefrontal cortex (fingertips folded over thumb)
     manages the limbic system (thumb) and brainstem (palm).
   - "Flipping your lid" = when the prefrontal cortex disengages from the limbic system under stress.

"Where attention goes, neural firing flows, and neural connection grows."

"Integration — the linkage of differentiated elements — is the basis
of health. When integration is impaired, we move toward chaos or rigidity."
— The Developing Mind

"The best predictor of a child's security of attachment is not what happened
to their parents as children, but rather whether their parents have made
sense of what happened to them."
— Parenting from the Inside Out (2003)

"Mindsight is a kind of focused attention that allows us to see the
internal workings of our own minds. It helps us become aware of our mental
processes without being swept away by them."
— Mindsight

"Name it to tame it."

"When we attune to others, we allow our own internal state to shift,

"Secure attachment is the basis from which a child — and later an adult —

"You can't stop the waves, but you can learn to surf."

ON ATTACHMENT AND EFT-RELEVANT THEMES:

"When a person feels felt — when they sense that another person has truly
grasped their internal experience — something profound happens in the brain.
The nervous system settles, and the person can move from reactivity to receptivity."

"The window of tolerance is the zone of arousal in which a person is able
to function most effectively. When people are within this zone, they are
typically able to readily receive, process, and integrate information."

"Earned security is one of the most hopeful findings in attachment research —
it shows that reflection and relationship can transform insecure attachment
into security."

"We are hardwired to connect. Our nervous systems are designed to be
regulated by the presence of attuned others."
