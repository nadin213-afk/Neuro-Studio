SUE JOHNSON — Key Ideas & Quotes
Founder of Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)
==============================================

CORE IDEAS:

1. Attachment as the foundation of adult love
   - Adult romantic love is an attachment bond, not just an emotion or a behavior.
   - The need for safe emotional connection with a few irreplaceable others is wired in by evolution.

2. The negative cycle
   - Distressed couples get caught in rigid patterns (pursue-withdraw, attack-attack).
   - The cycle, not the partner, is the enemy.
   - "The problem is never really about the content of the fight — it's about the disconnection."

3. Accessibility, Responsiveness, Engagement (A.R.E.)
   - The core question in every relationship: "Are you there for me?"
   - A.R.E. = Are you Accessible? Are you Responsive? Are you Engaged?

4. Primary vs. secondary emotions
   - Anger and criticism are secondary, reactive emotions.
   - Underneath lies fear, sadness, loneliness — the primary attachment emotions.
   - Healing happens when partners can share their softer, primary emotions.

5. Hold Me Tight conversations
   - The antidote to disconnection is reaching for the partner from vulnerability.

KEY QUOTES:

"The message of EFT is simple: Forget about learning how to argue better,
analyzing your early childhood, or making grand romantic gestures. Instead,
recognize and admit that you are emotionally attached to and dependent on
your partner in much the same way that a child is on a parent for nurturing,
soothing, and protection."
— Hold Me Tight (2008)

"Love is not the icing on the cake of life. It is a basic biological necessity."
— Hold Me Tight

"Are you there for me? Will you come when I call? Do I matter to you?
Am I valued and accepted by you? Do you need me? Will you respond
when I reach for you?"
— Hold Me Tight

"The bonds of love offer us a safe haven — a place of comfort and security
from which to face the world."
— Love Sense (2013)

"Emotional isolation is traumatizing for human beings. The brain codes it
as danger."
— Love Sense

"When we feel safely linked to our partner, we can tolerate the hurts
they inflict and the ambiguity that is part of every relationship."
— Hold Me Tight

"In EFT we do not teach communication skills. We help people find
the emotions under the emotions, and when they speak from that place,
they naturally communicate differently."
— Created for Connection (2016)

"The number one thing that defines a good relationship is emotional
responsiveness."
— Interview, 2015

"Attachment theory teaches us that our loved one is our shelter in life.
When that person is unavailable or unresponsive, we are in danger."
— Hold Me Tight

"We are bonding animals who need a very particular kind of connection —
one where we feel safe enough to be vulnerable."
— Love Sense

EFT MODEL — Three Stages:
Stage 1: De-escalation — identify the negative cycle
Stage 2: Restructuring the bond — access deeper emotions, create new bonding events
Stage 3: Consolidation — solidify new patterns of connection
